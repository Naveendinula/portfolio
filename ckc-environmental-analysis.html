Abstract

This comprehensive environmental performance analysis examines the CKC Building in Central Hong Kong's dense urban context. Using parametric environmental simulation tools and 8,760-hour climate data, the study evaluates weather patterns, solar exposure, thermal comfort conditions, and visual connections to inform sustainable design strategies for the subtropical climate.

Introduction

  • Context: CKC Building represents a critical urban redevelopment opportunity in Hong Kong's Central district.
  • Objective: Quantify environmental performance metrics to guide sustainable design decisions in a high-density tropical context.
  • Approach: Multi-scale analysis combining microclimate assessment, solar studies, and urban comfort evaluation.

Site Context & Location Analysis

Located in Central Hong Kong, the CKC site sits within one of the world's densest urban environments. The 1 km² analysis area encompasses the Victoria Harbour coastline, Central Park green spaces, and surrounding high-rise developments, creating a complex microclimate with significant shading, wind channeling, and urban heat island effects.

Direct Sun Analysis

1. Purpose

  • Quantify how much direct solar exposure each floor façade receives through the year.
  • Compare annual vs. key seasonal periods (March equinox, June solstice, December solstice).
  • Flag high-exposure façade zones (design for shading / glazing performance).
  • Understand usable sun in adjacent public open spaces (Cheung Kong Park, Chater Garden).

Solar Radiation Analysis

1. Purpose & Methodology

While direct sun analysis quantifies hours of solar exposure, radiation analysis calculates the actual energy flux (kWh m⁻²) incident on building surfaces. This approach uses sophisticated sky modeling to assess both beneficial winter solar gains and problematic summer cooling loads, informing glazing specifications and shading strategies.

Matrix Analysis Approach
Matrix What it contains Why we need it
Sky Matrix Absolute annual kWh m⁻² from each of the 580 Tregenza sky patches. Baseline resource: feeds incident‑radiation studies and radiation roses.
Benefit Matrix Same sky dome, but values carry a sign: + = useful winter gain, – = harmful summer load (computed from hourly outdoor temperature). Instantly tells us whether sun on a surface helps reduce heating or worsens cooling.

Design implications

Hong Kong's subtropical climate means most solar radiation represents a cooling penalty rather than heating benefit. West and south facades show predominantly negative energy balance, requiring aggressive shading strategies. Only winter morning sun on east facades shows consistent positive benefit for office buildings.

View Analysis

1. Purpose & Methodology — View / Sky Exposure Study

This study measures how well occupants of the Cheung Kong Center connect to their surroundings—Victoria Harbour, nearby parks, and the open sky—and how that connection changes with height. Using Grasshopper view‑analysis tools, façade grids were tested for:

  • Harbour / landscape visibility – % of each panel with an unobstructed ocean horizon.
  • Sky exposure – fraction of visible sky dome per point (blue → high, green → low in the diagrams).
  • Obstruction mapping – neighbouring towers that block key sightlines and daylight.
  • Vertical gradient – how views and sky access improve from podium to roof.

Results guide window placement, glazing priorities, and interior programming to maximize wellbeing and capitalize on Hong Kong's harbour and skyline vistas.
